SLIDE 5: MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATIONS
================================================================================
Content Type: Equations + Explanation

Loss Functions:
1. Cross-Entropy Loss: L_CE = -Σ_i y_i * log(ŷ_i)
   - Derived from Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) with categorical distribution
   - Objective: Minimize KL divergence between true and predicted distributions

2. Mean Squared Error (MSE): L_MSE = Σ_i (y_i - ŷ_i)²
   - Alternative derived from MLE with Gaussian likelihood
   - Less suitable for classification but used for comparison in foundations phase

Regularization Terms:
- L1 Penalty: λ * Σ |w|  (Lasso, encourages sparsity)
- L2 Penalty: λ * Σ w²   (Ridge, discourages large weights)
- Dropout Regularizer: Stochastic neuron dropping (p=0.5) at training time
- Batch Normalization: Reduces internal covariate shift via z-score normalization
- Label Smoothing: Replaces hard targets y with soft targets y' = (1-ε)*y + ε/K

SLIDE 19: KEY FINDINGS & CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================
Content Type: Numbered Findings + Implications

Finding 1: Combination Methods Dominate
  → Dropout + BatchNorm outperforms single methods by 0.3-1.1%
  → Implies complementary regularization mechanisms
  → Practical Takeaway: Combine orthogonal techniques for best results

Finding 2: Sparsity ≠ Accuracy
  → L1 induces 39% sparsity but ranks 9th in accuracy
  → Data Augmentation lowest accuracy (84.92%) but best generalization gap
  → Implies: Sparsity and accuracy are different optimization objectives; choose based on goal

Finding 3: BatchNorm Beats Dropout in Single-Method Context
  → BatchNorm 88.72% vs Dropout 88.62% (single methods)
  → But combined with Dropout, further gains achieved (89.03%)
  → Implies: Interaction effects matter; test combinations systematically

Finding 4: Adaptive Optimizers Converge Significantly Faster
  → AdamW reaches target accuracy in 36 epochs vs 65 for SGD
  → 80% reduction in training time without accuracy cost
  → Practical Takeaway: Use adaptive methods for large-scale projects; tune momentum for SGD if computational budget tight

Finding 5: Warmup + Cosine Scheduling Brings Consistent Gains
  → +0.68% accuracy improvement over constant learning rate
  → Minimal implementation cost, universally beneficial
  → Practical Takeaway: Include cosine annealing in production pipelines

Finding 6: Adversarial Robustness Requires Specialized Training
  → Standard regularization provides ~3-5% robustness boost (marginal)
  → Trade-offs: 2-5% clean accuracy loss for true robustness (not studied here)
  → Practical Takeaway: For sensitive applications, incorporate adversarial training; standard regularization insufficient

Finding 7: Generalization Gap Diagnostic
  → Smallest gaps (Data Augmentation 0.18, Dropout+BN 0.19) indicate excellent generalization
  → Largest gaps (Baseline 0.42) show uncontrolled overfitting
  → Practical Takeaway: Monitor gap as proxy for test performance; early stopping using this metric plausible

Questions to Anticipate:
Q: Why Dropout + BatchNorm over L2 + Dropout?
A: Complementary mechanisms—Dropout masks neurons, BatchNorm normalizes activations; together they address overfitting from different angles.

Q: Can results transfer to other datasets?
A: Maybe partially. MLP-specific findings may not hold for CNNs; recommend testing on ImageNet/CIFAR.

Q: How sensitive is the ranking to seed choice?
A: Low sensitivity—3 seeds show <0.5% variance in mean accuracy for most methods; outliers noted in results tables.

Q: Why not use more seeds (e.g., 10)?
A: Computational budget; recommended future work to increase confidence intervals.

Q: Is FGSM robustness sufficient for production?
A: No—FGSM single-step; recommend PGD multi-step adversarial training for robust models.
